Explore Pakistan

Top Destinations

Murree

The Queen of Hills — famous for snow-clad peaks, pine forests, and colonial architecture.

Nathia Gali

A serene hill station surrounded by cedar trees, ideal for hiking and cool summer escapes.

Skardu

Gateway to the Karakoram — home to K2 base camps, Shigar Fort, and turquoise lakes.

Hunza

A peaceful valley surrounded by mountains, known for its apricot orchards and hospitality.

Lahore

The cultural heart of Pakistan — filled with Mughal architecture, gardens, and rich cuisine.

Karachi

Pakistan’s vibrant port city offering beaches, nightlife, and a mix of cultures.

Multan

The City of Saints — famous for its Sufi shrines, blue pottery, and mangoes.

Must-Try Pakistani Foods

  • 🍛 Biryani – a flavorful rice dish cooked with aromatic spices and meat.
  • 🥘 Nihari – slow-cooked beef stew traditionally eaten for breakfast.
  • 🍢 Seekh Kebab – grilled skewers of spiced minced meat.
  • 🥩 Sajji – whole roasted lamb or chicken marinated with salt and spices.
  • 🥣 Haleem – a rich porridge of wheat, lentils, and meat cooked overnight.
  • 🍞 Halwa Puri – the perfect traditional breakfast combination.

Cultural Festivals & Activities

Pakistan’s festivals and cultural events reflect its diverse heritage and deep-rooted traditions:

  • Basant Festival – Kite-flying celebration marking the arrival of spring in Lahore.
  • Shandur Polo Festival – “The Roof of the World” hosts the highest polo ground on Earth.
  • Truck Art – Every truck is a moving canvas painted with colors and poetry.
  • Sufi Music Nights – Traditional Qawwali performances at shrines like Data Darbar and Sehwan.
  • Ajrak & Sindhi Topi Day – Celebrating Sindh’s textile and craftsmanship heritage.

Traditional Handicrafts

Explore the artisan skills that represent Pakistan’s cultural identity:

  • Blue Pottery of Multan
  • Ajrak Printing of Sindh
  • Peshawari Chappal Leatherwork
  • Marble Inlay and Brass Handicrafts of Punjab
  • Ralli Quilts and Embroidery from Rural Sindh
